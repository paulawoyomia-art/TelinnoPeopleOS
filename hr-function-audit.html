1. Employment & Engagement Process
Appointment letters · references · induction · ID · probation · handbook acknowledgement
HR Policy Handbook — Section 1
Is a formal letter of appointment issued to every new hire before or on their first day?
Policy (Section 1, HR Policy Handbook): "You will be given a letter of employment once you have been considered suitable for employment." Employment without a formal letter is not legally constituted and creates significant exposure in all operating jurisdictions.
Are 2 recent passport photographs (not older than 6 months) collected from all new employees on resumption?
Policy (Section 1, HR Policy Handbook): Required documentation on engagement. Also required: 3 references (1 from previous employer, 2 personal) — or 2 references if it is the employee's first job.
Is a structured induction programme conducted for new hires, ending with a signed induction form from both the employee and HR?
Policy (Section 1, HR Policy Handbook): "The HR unit coordinates the induction programme for a new employee. At the end of the induction programme, an induction form is filled and signed by the new employee and HR." The signed form is the compliance evidence.
Is an employment number and ID card issued to every employee? Are ID cards being worn in the office as required?
Policy (Section 1, HR Policy Handbook): "Every employee will be issued an employment number and employment ID card. The employment ID card must be worn at all times in the TCL office and shown upon request to customers."
Is probation correctly managed? 3-month period → written supervisor assessment → written confirmation letter. Extensions (max 6 months total) issued in writing?
Policy (Section 1, HR Policy Handbook): "You will remain on probation for an initial period of three (3) months… If the appraisal is satisfactory… you will receive a formal confirmation letter in writing." Extension allowed but cannot exceed 6 months total.
Has the HR Policy Handbook and Employee Handbook been issued to all employees? Has each employee signed and returned the acknowledgement slip?
Policy (Both Handbooks): Both the HR Policy Handbook ("Agreement to the Terms" page) and the Employee Handbook (Section C acknowledgement) require the employee to sign and return confirmation. Without this, the company cannot enforce the handbook's terms.

2. Compensation & Pay Administration
Payslips · bank accounts · salary structure · salary bands · pay equity
HR Policy Handbook — Section 2; Employee Handbook — Section B.9
Is a payslip (pay advice) issued to every employee on or before the last working day of every month?
Policy (Section 2, HR Policy Handbook): "Payday is the last working day every month… A pay advice will be issued to you." This is also a statutory requirement in Nigeria (CITA) and equivalent legislation in Mali, Niger, CIV, and Cameroon. Non-issuance is a regulatory violation.
Does every employee have a bank account on file? Is all salary paid by direct bank credit — no cash payments?
Policy (Section 2, HR Policy Handbook): "For the purpose of receiving your pay, you are expected to maintain an account with one of the company's banks… your account will be credited directly by the bank." Employee Handbook (Section B.9): "The Company's normal method of payment is by credit to bank account of Employees. (No cash payment)"
Is every employee's compensation package documented with a full breakdown: basic salary + housing allowance + transport allowance + meal allowance?
Policy (Section 2, HR Policy Handbook): "Your compensation package is made up of salary and benefits. Each position in the company has a range of basic salary and allowances. Allowances include housing, transport and meal." Each component must be individually documented per employee.
Is there a documented, market-benchmarked salary band for every role type in your KAD? Are pay decisions made within the band?
Policy (Section 2, HR Policy Handbook): "All salary ranges are reviewed periodically, to assure our continued competitiveness. The salary ranges are related to pay in the competitive community for specific levels of responsibility." Without bands, compensation decisions are arbitrary and create inequity claims.

3. Performance Management
Goals · written appraisals (twice yearly) · 1:1 cadence · PIP · consequence management
HR Policy Handbook — Sections 2 & 7; Employee Handbook — Section A.7
Do all employees in your KAD have documented, agreed performance goals for the current period — connected to KAD objectives?
Policy (Section 2, HR Policy Handbook): "Performance is the principle behind our reward system." Without documented goals, appraisals cannot be objective, promotion decisions are indefensible, and compensation reviews have no basis. Goals must be written — verbal agreements are insufficient.
Have written performance appraisals been conducted in the last 6 months? (Policy mandates twice yearly — mid-year and end-of-year, both in writing)
Policy (Section 2, HR Policy Handbook): "You will receive written performance appraisals twice a year (every 6 months – mid and end-of-year). These appraisals will be used to determine the amount and timing of your merit or promotional increases." Written — not verbal. On file — not forgotten.
Are regular performance conversations (1:1s) happening between managers and their direct reports at a defined cadence?
PVC Doctrine (Phase 5): Continuous feedback is the operating mechanism between formal appraisals. A 6-month appraisal cycle without ongoing 1:1s means feedback arrives 6 months too late to change behaviour. The 1:1 is the manager's most important management tool.
Is there a documented underperformance process (PIP) being applied correctly? Written warning first → PIP initiation → review → termination only after documented failure?
Policy (Section 7, HR Policy Handbook): "A below average performance during a scheduled performance appraisal will commence a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP), after a written warning for non performance has been issued… if performance still falls below average after the PIP, the Line manager may give another written warning… thereafter if no improvement, advise management to effect termination."

4. Discipline & Grievance Management
Query process · written responses · warnings · suspension · grievance procedure · due process
HR Policy Handbook — Sections 5 & 6; Employee Handbook — Section B.19
Is the correct disciplinary sequence followed: query issued → employee given 24 hours to respond in writing → management action → escalation if repeated?
Policy (Section 6, HR Policy Handbook): "When you commit any offence… you may receive a query from the Human Resources unit. You will be giving an opportunity to state your own case in writing within 24 hours." 3 queries in a year → suspension without pay. 4th query → termination. This process must be followed exactly for any termination to be legally defensible.
Are all disciplinary actions (queries, warnings, suspensions) documented in writing and filed in the employee's HR record?
Policy (Section 6, HR Policy Handbook): Verbal queries and verbal warnings are not legally enforceable. Every query must be in writing, every response in writing, every outcome in writing. Without a documented trail, the company cannot defend a termination decision in any jurisdiction.
Is there a documented grievance procedure that employees know about and can access? Is the 2-step escalation (supervisor → HR within 3 days) being followed?
Policy (Section 5, HR Policy Handbook): "If you need to do so, you must use the following problem review: 1. First discuss the problem with your immediate supervisor. 2. If you have not received a satisfactory response after 3 days, then you may pass the matter to HR for final settlement." If employees don't know this process exists, they either escalate inappropriately or suppress legitimate concerns.

5. Leave Management
Annual leave entitlement · leave schedules · sick leave certification · maternity/paternity
HR Policy Handbook — Section 3; Employee Handbook — Section B.16
Is each employee's annual leave entitlement stated in their letter of employment? Do employees know their exact entitlement?
Policy (Section 3, HR Policy Handbook): "Your leave entitlement for each year is as stated in your letter of employment." A generic 'refer to policy' is insufficient — leave entitlement must be individually specified in each employment letter. Eligibility requires appointment confirmation and at least 12 months continuous service.
Have leave schedules been submitted to HR by all employees for this year? Is a consolidated leave plan maintained for your KAD?
Policy (Section 3, HR Policy Handbook): "You are required to submit your annual leave date to the Human Resources unit by January 31 each year. A leave schedule will be compiled on a 'first on time' basis." Without a leave plan, the organisation has no visibility of planned absences and cannot prevent operational gaps.
Is sick leave tracked against the 24-day annual cap? Is a medical certificate from a registered practitioner required and collected?
Policy (Section 3, HR Policy Handbook): "You shall be entitled to sick leave with full pay of 24 days in any year provided that the illness occasioning such leave is certified by a registered medical practitioner." Absence beyond 24 days without certification is unpaid and actionable under policy.
Is maternity leave (12 weeks paid, requires 12 months service) and paternity leave (3 days max per annum) being administered correctly and consistently?
Policy (Section 3, HR Policy Handbook): Maternity: 12 weeks full pay for confirmed employees with 12+ months service. Extension: 4 weeks unpaid at manager's discretion. Annual leave cannot be built into maternity leave. Paternity: maximum 3 days per annum. Inconsistent application creates discrimination claims.

6. Separations & Exit Process
30-day notice · clearance form · final pay · asset recovery · exit interview
HR Policy Handbook — Section 7; Employee Handbook — Section B.6
Is the full clearance procedure followed for all exits? (Line Manager → IT/Information Management → Admin → Finance → HR all sign off before final pay is released)
Policy (Section 7, HR Policy Handbook): "HR prepares a Clearance Form in three (3) copies. Employee approaches the Heads of each department: (a) Line Manager (b) Information Management (c) Administration (d) Finance (e) HR." All must sign before final pay computation. No shortcuts.
Is final pay computed within 1 month of termination? Is the 90%/10% retention applied? (10% held for 60 days for outstanding invoices, then released)
Policy (Section 7, HR Policy Handbook): "The Company pays 90% of the total net pay and holds 10% retention amount… After a period of not more than sixty (60) days, HR informs Finance to release the remaining 10%." Final pay must also include all earned salary, allowances, bonuses, and unused vacation leave.
Are all company assets recovered from leavers before exit? (ID card, CUG SIM, laptop, phone, access cards) Are exit interviews conducted?
Policy (Sections 1 & 2, HR Policy Handbook): ID card: "At the end of contract/employment, the employee must submit his/her identification card to the HR unit." CUG SIM: "On termination of employment, the Employee must return it to the HR Manager." Unrecovered assets are a cost and a security risk.

7. Recruitment & Selection
JDs for ALL existing roles · JSPs · structured selection · internal priority · pre-employment checks
HR Policy Handbook — Section 1; PVC Phases 2 & 3
Does every existing role in your KAD have a current, written Job Description — not just roles being hired for, but every role that currently exists?
Critical distinction: A Job Description must exist for every role that currently exists in the organisation — not just open vacancies. Without a JD, you cannot manage performance against a role that has not been defined, you cannot conduct a compliant appraisal, and you cannot build a JSP or competency framework. This is the most fundamental documentation gap in the system.
Does every existing role have a completed Job Success Profile (JSP) — defining purpose, outputs, competency requirements, and what excellent performance looks like?
PVC Phase 1 (Job Analysis): Without a success profile, "organisations write job descriptions based on what the previous person happened to be good at, not what the role actually requires. This produces misaligned hires, unclear performance expectations, and compensation inconsistencies." The JSP is the upgrade from JD — it answers not just 'what' but 'what does good look like'.
Is there a structured, scored interview and selection process for all hires above coordinator level? (Not gut feel, informal conversation, or manager preference)
PVC Phase 3 (Selection): "Unstructured interviews have a predictive validity of approximately 0.2 — barely better than chance for predicting job performance. Structured competency-based interviews have validities of 0.51–0.58." Using unstructured interviews is a deliberate choice to make expensive, poor-quality decisions. Pre-employment medical examination may also be required (Employee Handbook Section B.1).
Are internal candidates given priority consideration before any external search for vacancies? Is this actually happening in practice?
Policy (Section 1, HR Policy Handbook): "The company believes in filling vacant positions with the best-qualified persons available, after giving due consideration to promotable employees." Employee Handbook (Section A.6): "Employees who are considered by the Management as being qualified to fill vacancies in higher positions/grades within the organization will be given preference over external candidates."

8. Onboarding
First-day readiness · 30/60/90 day plans · role clarity · KAD integration · HRBP check-ins
HR Policy Handbook — Section 1 (Induction); PVC Phase 4
Do new hires receive a structured 30/60/90-day onboarding plan — not just admin setup and access, but actual integration into the KAD?
PVC Phase 4: "15–20% of new hires report doubts within the first week. The organisation that makes a new hire feel expected, welcomed, and prepared is making an investment with a high return." Onboarding is not just administration — it is the first test of the employer's promise.
Do new hires (and employees post-restructure) have documented clarity on their KAD assignment, reporting line, performance expectations, and who they are accountable to — within week 1?
Policy context + Implementation Plan: Post-April 2026 restructure, every employee must have received formal written confirmation of their KAD, reporting line, and role. Verbal communication of structure is insufficient. Ambiguity in week 1 drives disengagement that is almost impossible to reverse.

9. Learning, Development & Promotions
Competency framework · training needs analysis · IDPs · merit-based promotions
HR Policy Handbook — Section 2; Employee Handbook — Section A.6 & A.7
Does a competency framework exist for every role in your KAD? Has a Training Needs Analysis been conducted using the Competency Matrix tool?
PVC Phase 5 (LNA): "L&D without LNA produces learning activity rather than capability development. Training budgets are consumed by programmes that feel good but address the wrong skills, at the wrong level, for the wrong people." The Competency Matrix tool on this portal is the mechanism for this.
Do employees have Individual Development Plans (IDPs) connected to identified competency gaps and career aspirations?
Policy (Declaration, HR Policy Handbook): "Provide career development opportunities and support, encourage and assist every employee further develop a service-oriented career by providing career training." This is a stated company commitment — the absence of IDPs is a breach of it.
Is promotion merit-based, connected to documented appraisal outcomes, and applied consistently? Is internal mobility actively facilitated across KADs?
Policy (Section 2, HR Policy Handbook): "The company's policy on promotion is merit based and performance oriented. Emphasis is also placed on the bi-annual performance appraisal." Employee Handbook (Section A.6): "Promotions to higher grades shall be by merit, subject to existence of vacancies." Undocumented or subjective promotion decisions create perceived unfairness and legal risk.

10. HR Data & People Records
Master employee register · HRIS · reporting capability · workforce plan
HR Policy Handbook — Sections 1 & 8; PVC Phase 9
Do you have a reliable, complete, and current master record of all employees in your KAD — not fragmented across spreadsheets, email threads, and WhatsApp messages?
Policy (Section 1, HR Policy Handbook): "All new employees shall provide HR with full and accurate information required for administrative procedures and statutory requirements. Staff records and related correspondence shall be held in confidential status at all times." Confidential records in WhatsApp are not compliant.
Can you produce a headcount, attrition, or vacancy report for your KAD within 24 hours if asked by the CEO?
PVC Phase 9: "If the HRBP cannot pull a BU attrition report without raising a ticket, the analytics capability is not serving the people who need it most." Basic reporting capability is not optional — it is the minimum standard for a functioning HRBP.
Does your KAD have a documented workforce plan aligned to 2026 KAD business objectives — covering planned hires, capability gaps, and make/buy/borrow decisions?
PVC Phase 1 (Workforce Planning): "Without workforce planning, organisations hire reactively — filling vacancies rather than building capability. The result is a workforce perpetually one step behind the business, with chronic skills gaps, bottlenecks in critical functions, and no pipeline for future leaders."

11. Employee Engagement & Relations
Post-restructure transition · flight risk · morale · ER concerns · listening channels
PVC Phases 7 & 8; Implementation Plan — Month 1
Have you completed 1:1 transition conversations with all employees in your KAD post-restructure? Does every person know their KAD, their leader, and their reporting line?
Implementation Plan (Month 1, Priority): "HRBP priority: complete individual transition conversations with every person in their KAD." Without this, structural ambiguity from the April 2026 restructure remains unresolved. Ambiguity is a direct driver of disengagement and departure.
Have you identified any flight risks in your KAD post-restructure? Have retention conversations been initiated for named individuals?
PVC Phase 8 (Retention): "Restructures are the highest-risk moment for voluntary departure — particularly among people who were not consulted and who have options. Retention decisions made proactively — not reactively after a resignation." If you know someone is at risk and have not initiated a conversation, the resignation is already in progress.
What is your honest assessment of overall morale in your KAD right now — post-restructure?
Note: This is a direct HRBP observation, not a survey metric. Your assessment is the most current intelligence the organisation has. Name what you are seeing.

12. HRBP Mandate & Operating Effectiveness
Operating charter · weekly sync · tools & resources · biggest blocker
Implementation Plan — HRBP Mandates Section
Do you have a written HRBP Operating Charter — defining what you own, what you decide independently, what you escalate to the HR lead?
Implementation Plan: "Each HRBP must have a signed operating charter." Without it, the HRBP's authority is unclear, escalation paths are undefined, and the organisation cannot hold the HRBP accountable — or protect them when they need backing from senior leadership.
Is there a regular weekly or fortnightly HRBP sync with the HR lead and/or CEO?
Implementation Plan: "The weekly HRBP sync is the connective tissue of the HR operating model. Without it, each HRBP operates in isolation, cross-KAD talent opportunities are missed, ER decisions are made inconsistently, and the CEO has no integrated view of the people system."
Do you have the tools, templates, access, and budget you need to do your job effectively?
Implementation Plan: An HRBP without tools is a title without function. This question is not about the HRBP improvising better — it is about what the organisation needs to invest to make the HRBP role functional.
